How to Use This

Adding entries during the week

Tap "Add Entry" on the main screen. You'll go through 3 steps: pick a day, pick or type a task type, then fill in the details and hours. Tap "Save Entry" when you're done. You can add as many entries as you need throughout the week.

Editing or deleting

Each entry has Edit and Delete links underneath it. Edit takes you back to the details step so you can change anything. Delete removes it straight away.

The summary

The summary shows your total hours, how many are claimed (capped at your max — set this in Settings), and any time owing. "Time Owed" entries are subtracted before calculating.

Custom tasks

On the task step, pick a built-in type or type your own. Custom tasks are remembered and appear as buttons next time. To remove one you no longer need, tap the ✕ next to it on the task step — this only removes it from the list, any existing entries using it stay put.

Exporting

At the end of the week, tap "Export for Excel." The iOS share sheet opens — the quickest path is usually:

Tap Mail to send it to yourself or someone else. A new email opens with the timesheet already attached as a CSV file.

Or tap Save to Files, pick On My iPhone or iCloud Drive, then open the Files app, tap the file, tap Share, and choose Copy to Excel or Copy to Numbers.

If Excel or Numbers appear directly in the share sheet (scroll down past the apps row to check), you can tap them from there too — but this depends on your iOS version, and Save to Files always works.

On a computer

On a desktop browser the export downloads as a CSV file instead of opening the share sheet. Open it in Excel or Numbers from your Downloads folder.

Settings

In Settings you can change your maximum weekly hours. The default is 7.
