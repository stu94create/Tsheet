How to Use This

Adding entries during the week

Tap "Add Entry" on the main screen. You'll go through 3 steps: pick a day, pick or type a task type, then fill in the details and hours. Tap "Save Entry" when you're done. You can add as many entries as you need throughout the week.

Editing or deleting

Each entry has Edit and Delete links underneath it. Edit takes you back to the details step so you can change anything. Delete removes the entry but gives you 5 seconds to tap Undo and get it back. The same applies when you clear all entries for a week.

The summary

The summary shows your total hours, how many are claimed (capped at your max — set this in Settings), and any time owing for the week.

Custom tasks

On the task step, pick a built-in type or type your own. Custom tasks are remembered and appear as buttons next time. To remove one you no longer need, tap the ✕ next to it on the task step — this only removes it from the list, any existing entries using it stay put.

Exporting your timesheet

At the end of the week, tap "Export Timesheet." The export is a proper Excel file (.xlsx) with headers, column widths, borders and a shaded totals row — so it opens looking tidy in Excel or Numbers without any manual formatting.

The iOS share sheet opens. Tap Save to Files, pick a location like On My iPhone or iCloud Drive, and tap Save.

Then open the Files app, find the file where you saved it, and tap it. Tap the Share button (the square with the arrow pointing up), and choose Copy to Excel or Copy to Numbers. The file opens straight into the app ready to view, edit, email or send on.

If you just want to email it to someone, you can also tap Mail directly from the first share sheet — a new email opens with the file already attached.

On a computer

On a desktop browser the export downloads an .xlsx file to your Downloads folder. Double-tap it to open in Excel or Numbers.

Time off and sick leave

To log time off or sick leave, choose "Time Off" or "Sick Leave" as the task type when adding an entry. In Settings you can set an allowance for each (in hours). The app tracks how much you've used across all weeks and shows how much remains on the main screen. If you don't set an allowance, you'll still see a running total of hours taken.

Hours owing

If you work more than your weekly max, the extra hours are "owing." The app tracks this across all your saved weeks and shows the cumulative total on the main screen. To use your owed hours, add an entry with "Hours Reclaimed" as the task type. The app subtracts reclaimed hours from your total and shows both the earned and used amounts.

Backup and restore

In Settings, tap "Download Backup" to save all your entries, custom tasks and settings to a file. You can save it to iCloud Drive, email it to yourself, or keep it on your phone. To restore from a backup (e.g. on a new phone), tap "Choose Backup File" and pick the file. This replaces your current data.

Settings

In Settings you can change your maximum weekly hours (default 7), set your time off and sick leave allowances, and back up or restore your data.
